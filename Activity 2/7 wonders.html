7 WONDERS OF THE WORLD

The Seven Wonders of the World is a compiled list of monuments from all over the world that excel in unique architecture, craftsmanship and ingenuity. The included catalogue includes the best natural wonders or human-built monuments over time. The list has been modified and changed over time, but here is the latest compilation of the seven wonders of the world as of 2021.

TAJ MAHAL, INDIA

ASIA

An immense mausoleum of white marble, built in Agra between 1631 and 1648 by order of the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his favourite wife.

GREAT WALL OF CHINA, CHINA

ASIA

The Great Wall of China is an ancient wall in China. The wall is made of cement, rocks, bricks, and dirt. It was finished in 1878.

PETRA, JORDAN

ASIA

Petra is half-built, half-carved into the rock, and is surrounded by mountains riddled with passages and gorges. One of the most famous aechaelogical sites.

MACHU PICCHU, PERU

NORTH AMERICA

Machu Picchu is a 15th-century Inca site located on a ridge between the Huayna Picchu and Machu Picchu mountains in Peru.

THE COLOSSEU, ROME 

EUROPE

Colosseum, also called Flavian Amphitheatre, giant amphitheatre built in Rome under the Flavian emperors. Construction of the Colosseum was begun  70 and 72 CE.

CHRIST THE REDEEMER, BRAZIL

SOUTH AMERICA

 Christ the Redeemer is the largest art deco statue in the world. It is 98 feet tall (not including the 26 foot pedestal), and the arms stretch to 92 feet wide.

CHICHEN ITZA,MEXICO

NORTH AMERICA 

Chichén Itzá, ruined ancient Maya city occupying an area of 4 square miles (10 square km) in south-central Yucatán state, Mexico.
